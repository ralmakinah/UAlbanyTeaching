Recap: What Comes under Artificial Intelligence?

What does (Intellegence) mean?

  • to acquire, understand, and apply knowledge and skills to adapt to new situations, solve problems, and achieve goals.
  • the mental capacity for reasoning, planning, problem-solving, abstract thinking, learning quickly, and adapting to change.
  • the ability to navigate real-world challenges, make good decisions, and handle social situations effectively.
  • in machines, intelligence is the ability to perform tasks that normally require human thinking, such as recognizing patterns, learning from data, or making decisions.

Origins

Alan Turing

  • The Turing Machine (1936) - the conceptual basis for all modern computing
  • World War II Cyrptanalysis - Enigma - the decoding of Nazi military codes

Cont.

  • The Turing Test - Determine if an agent is an AI or a human
  • The human side of Alan Turing
  • 2014 - machine passd the test!
  • 2018 - CEO of Google called a hairdresser using machine

Origins cont.

McCulloch and Pitts (1943) | neurophysiologists

  • Logic can explain the power of the brain.
  • Model neurons as simple on/off logical units (binary threshold devices).
  • Forms the basis of much of modern machine learning, neural networks, and computational neuroscience.

Wieners Cybernetics (1948) | mathematician

  • Cybernetics = the study of control, communication, and feedback loops in systems, whether biological, mechanical, or social.
  • Central concepts: feedback loops, information theory, and self-regulation.
  • Unified ideas from biology, engineering, and mathematics into a new science of information and control.

Origins cont.

The Chinese Room

  • John Searle (1980)
  • Doing an intelligent task by rote
